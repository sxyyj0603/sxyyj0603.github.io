Working Paper

  • A Model of Optimal QE and QT with Segmented Bond Market and Liquidity Regulations.

    Abstract: This paper analyzes optimal asset-purchase and exit policies in a macroeconomic model with banks facing precautionary liquidity regulations (Liquidity Stress Testing and Leverage Coverage Ratio) and balance sheet costs. QE accelerates recoveries after productivity, liquidity, and bank net worth shocks by inflating bond prices and restoring bank net worth. It becomes more powerful as it provides reserves and boosts intermediation when the LCR constraint binds. The optimal exit strategy is gradual whenever the LCR constraint binds. However, a fast QT is costless and expansionary when liquidity is abundant as it drives the banks to substitute bonds for working capital.
